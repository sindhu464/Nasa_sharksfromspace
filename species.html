Shark Species

An overview of the diverse and fascinating sharks we study and protect.

Great White Shark

Carcharodon carcharias

The world's largest predatory fish, famous for its size and power. Found in cool, coastal waters worldwide.

Up to 6m

Tiger Shark

Galeocerdo cuvier

A formidable macropredator with a very broad diet, named for the dark, vertical stripes found on juveniles.

Up to 5m

Whale Shark

Rhincodon typus

The largest known living fish. This gentle giant is a filter-feeder, primarily eating plankton and small fish.

Up to 18m

Basking Shark

Cetorhinus maximus

The second-largest living shark. It is a slow-moving filter-feeder, often seen swimming with its enormous mouth open.

Up to 12m

Hammerhead Shark

Sphyrnidae

Instantly recognizable by their unique "hammer" shaped head (cephalofoil), which gives them superior sensory perception.

Up to 6m

Blue Shark

Prionace glauca

A slim, elegant shark with a distinct indigo blue color on its back. They are found in deep waters in temperate and tropical oceans.

Up to 3.8m

Goblin Shark

Mitsukurina owstoni

A rare deep-sea shark, often called a "living fossil," with a distinctive elongated snout and highly protrusible jaws.

Up to 4m

Bull Shark

Carcharhinus leucas

Known for its aggressive nature and ability to thrive in both saltwater and freshwater, often traveling far up rivers.

Up to 3.4m
