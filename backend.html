Shark Habitat Prediction Framework

An advanced analysis leveraging satellite data from NASA's SWOT and PACE missions to model and predict shark behavior.

Integrated Foraging Model

This comprehensive dashboard combines multiple data sources to predict shark foraging hotspots. It uses a Hidden Markov Model (HMM) to classify behavior into "Foraging" or "Transit" states. The Environmental Feature Importance (EFI) chart reveals that factors like hour of day and water depth are key predictors.

Foraging Hotspots (KDE)

This map shows a Kernel Density Estimation (KDE) of shark locations. The intense red and black areas signify "hotspots" where sharks congregate and likely spend the most time foraging.

Predictive Model Trajectories

This visualization displays the intricate network of trajectories considered by the predictive model before determining a shark's likely future location. Each line represents a possible path, illustrating the complex probabilistic analysis underlying the movement predictions.

Foraging Suitability Index

The Foraging Suitability Index (FSI) predicts the best feeding locations. This model integrates environmental data to create a probability map, with yellow areas indicating the most suitable foraging grounds.

Chlorophyll-a & Feeding Events

This visualization overlays shark feeding events on a map of Chlorophyll-a concentration. It shows a strong correlation between feeding and areas rich in phytoplankton, the base of the marine food web.

Regional Occupancy Map

This map details the general areas sharks inhabit. It provides a broader view of their range and movement corridors over long periods, complementing the specific hotspot analysis.

FSI with Tracks & Feeds

This rich visualization combines the FSI map with actual shark tracks and feeding events. This direct comparison helps validate the model's accuracy, confirming that sharks frequently feed in areas predicted to be highly suitable.

Feeding Events Timeline

This chart plots confirmed feeding events over time. Analyzing temporal patterns can reveal daily, monthly, or seasonal trends in foraging behavior, often linked to prey availability or environmental cycles.

Mean Chlorophyll at Shark Locations Over Time

This graph illustrates the average Chlorophyll-a concentration at the precise locations where sharks were detected, plotted over various time steps. It highlights how shark presence correlates with areas of higher primary productivity in the ocean.
